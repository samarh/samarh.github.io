I'm a researcher at the Center for Language Engineering in Lahore, Pakistan, where I work on building natural language processing tools for regional Pakistani language, with a focus on Urdu. My research interests centre around machine learning and its applications, an interest that was sparked when I took Andrew Ng's Coursera Machine Learning course back in the summer of 2014. I have been avidly reading literature and following current trends in the field ever since. While I work on natural language processing now, in the past I have also employed machine learning for computer vision.

I recieved my Bachelor's in Computer Science from the University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore, in 2016. My senior thesis was on using deep convolutional neural networks for learning representations from faces in order to predict facial keypoints. Other academic projects include a vehicle license plate recognition system, a handwritten digit recognizer, and a study of soccer statistics using data mining.

Prior to that, I did my O' and A' Levels at the Lahore Grammar School in 2010 and 2012, respectively. There, I served as the President of the I.T. Society and was on the Student Council and the Yearbook Editorial Board.

In my spare time I enjoy reading, sketching, and playing table tennis.
