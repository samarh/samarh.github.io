I'm a researcher at the Center for Language Engineering in Lahore, Pakistan, where I work on building natural language processing tools for Urdu, Pakistan's national language. My research interests centre around machine learning and its applications, an interest that was sparked by Andrew Ng's Stanford course in the summer of 2014. I have been avidly reading literature and following current trends in the field ever since. While I work on natural language processing now, in the past I have also employed machine learning for computer vision.

I recieved my Bachelor's in Computer Science from the University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore, in 2016. My senior thesis was on using deep convolutional neural for learning representations from faces, with a focus on predicting facial keypoints.
